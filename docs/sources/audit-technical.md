# Technical syllabus and official-source audit

Audit date: **2026-07-13**

Course model: **Spanish ULM(A/MAF) theory first, followed by a Part-FCL PPL(A) extension in this open course**

## Decision and source hierarchy

The ULM foundation is the official AESA MAF syllabus `FOR-ULM-P01-ETI01-GU01 Ed.01`. For the architecture of this open course, the later PPL(A) extension highlights applicable content in EASA `AMC1 FCL.210; FCL.215` that is absent or materially less detailed in MAF and revisits shared content according to the learner's demonstrated gaps. This editorial crosswalk is **not Part-FCL credit** and does not by itself shorten or waive an approved DTO/ATO course, its completion requirements or the required examinations. A PPL(A) applicant must complete every applicable element of the DTO/ATO's approved or declared Part-FCL programme and meet FCL/AMC requirements (or an approved alternative means of compliance); prior MAF material affects delivery only if and to the extent that the DTO/ATO incorporates or accepts it within that programme under the applicable authority rules.

For course governance, the precedence order is:

1. applicable EU and Spanish law, the EASA Part-FCL AMC syllabus, and current AESA material;
2. the aircraft's approved/current AFM or POH and its supplements, then current manufacturer instructions for the installed engine and equipment;
3. current AEMET/ICAO operational meteorological material;
4. EASA safety-promotion material and FAA handbooks for explanatory teaching only.

FAA material is not authority for EU/Spanish law, airspace, phraseology, licensing, operational minima, or weather-service practice. A manufacturer's manual for one model, serial-number range, installation, or revision is never a generic ULM limit.

### Editorial diagnostic rule for shared content

For every applicable AMC row, the open-course authors may tag the item `MAF-overlap`, `MAF-partial`, or `PPL-new`. These are **editorial sequencing labels only**: `MAF-overlap` receives a diagnostic and focused revision in this publication, `MAF-partial` receives targeted remediation, and `PPL-new` receives a full follow-on lesson. None of the labels establishes Part-FCL equivalence, credit, a reduced syllabus or permission to skip instruction. The DTO/ATO—not this publication—must determine delivery and completion evidence under its approved or declared programme; unless a specific applicable rule and the competent process provide credit, the applicant must complete all applicable FCL/AMC programme elements (or approved alternative means of compliance) and the required examinations. Any diagnostic/crosswalk record used by a DTO/ATO must be incorporated and accepted within that organisation's programme. Subject-name similarity alone is never evidence of legal credit.

## Boundary between general theory and aircraft/equipment manuals

| Layer | Appropriate course content | Content that must remain manual-specific |
|---|---|---|
| General theory | Physical principles, terminology, variables, causal relationships, generic calculation method, hazard recognition and how to find controlled data | No aircraft limit, speed, mass, CG envelope, performance result, configuration, fluid, checklist action or maintenance interval |
| Aircraft AFM/POH and supplements | Demonstrate how to identify revision status, limitations, loading data, performance charts and normal/abnormal/emergency sections | Actual V-speeds, wind/weight/configuration limits, mass and CG envelope, take-off/landing performance, fuel/reserve data, installed-system procedures and checklists |
| Rotax or other engine operator manual | Generic engine-cycle/system teaching may cite the current manual for the selected family | Model/variant/serial/installation-specific RPM, temperature, pressure, fuel/oil/coolant, carburettor-icing, starting/shutdown and abnormal procedures; reconcile with aircraft AFM/POH |
| BRS or other rescue-system manual | General activation physics, decision framing, post-deployment hazards, document/placard inspection | Installed system envelope, handle and activation procedure, limitations, inspection/repack/service life and placard/serial status; use aircraft supplement plus exact manufacturer manual |
| Avionics/equipment manual | General radio/GNSS/instrument principles, modes, database and integrity concepts | Buttonology, annunciations, failure modes, approved functions, database status and supplements for the installed make/model/software configuration |
| AD/SB/continuing-airworthiness records | Teach where and how status is checked | Never declare an individual aircraft compliant or serviceable from generic course material |

Where documents appear to conflict, stop and resolve the current approved hierarchy for the specific aircraft; do not average values or choose the more convenient number. A standalone engine or equipment manual cannot authorise an aircraft-level procedure.

## Dangerous simplifications to prohibit

- **“Air on top must meet air below at the trailing edge.”** Do not teach equal-transit-time. Explain pressure distribution and momentum/flow turning without inventing a timing rule.
- **“A stall is a low-speed event.”** Stall is primarily exceedance of critical angle of attack and can occur at any airspeed or attitude; configuration, loading, manoeuvre and contamination matter.
- **“Wind changes the aerodynamic stall speed.”** Keep IAS/CAS/TAS/GS and air-relative versus ground-relative motion distinct. Uniform wind changes track/groundspeed, not the wing's local stall condition.
- **“An ULM is simply a lighter PPL aeroplane.”** National legal status, certification basis, inertia, loading, control response, energy and installed systems differ. Do not transfer limits or procedures.
- **“FAA means accepted European procedure.”** FAA handbooks may explain stable science; U.S. law, airspace, phraseology, charts, services and weather products are not EASA/Spanish authority.
- **“A decoded METAR/TAF is a complete briefing.”** Include forecast uncertainty, warnings, GAMET/AIRMET/SIGMET as applicable, radar/satellite/lightning, route/alternate conditions, NOTAM and changes in time.
- **“The moving map is the airspace clearance.”** GNSS position and database depiction require currency, integrity/error awareness and cross-checking. They do not replace a clearance, current chart/AIP/NOTAM or lookout.
- **“A rule-of-thumb number is a limit.”** Never publish a universal crosswind, reserve, CG, runway factor, density-altitude correction, climb or landing value in place of law and the current AFM/POH.
- **“All Rotax 912s use the same limits and fluids.”** Family names hide variant, certification, installation and revision differences. Teach lookup discipline, not memorised generic values.
- **“All ballistic parachutes have one activation height/speed/checklist.”** Use the exact aircraft supplement, installed-system manual, placard and serial/revision status. Decision discussion is not a universal deployment command.
- **“Generic emergency memory items are safer.”** Do not create unapproved memory checklists. Separate immediate actions explicitly approved for the selected aircraft from subsequent checklist use and instructor training.
- **“A simple mass/stall/performance formula gives dispatch data.”** Formulas explain trends; the current AFM/POH, actual loading and environmental conditions provide operational data.
- **“Subject overlap or a good diagnostic score automatically grants PPL credit.”** AESA MAF coverage can inform the sequencing of this open course, but neither the crosswalk nor its diagnostic waives Part-FCL training/examinations or DTO/ATO completion duties. Only an express applicable credit provision accepted through the competent DTO/ATO/authority process can do so.
- **“Reading a spin leaflet authorises practice.”** Stall/spin/loss-of-control material supports recognition and prevention; manoeuvre and recovery training requires approved instruction and aircraft-specific limitations.
- **“A weather-guide screenshot is live weather.”** Static handbook examples and archived displays must be visibly labelled as training data and never reused for dispatch.

## Editorial and change-control concerns

- The AESA Ed.01 PDF does not print a calendar issue date and warns that downloaded copies are uncontrolled. Resolve and record the current AESA web revision at every course release.
- The AESA PDF repeats `CONOCIMIENTO GENERAL DE LA AERONAVE` as a heading on later pages that contain operational-procedures, human-performance and communications content. Map from the subject index and item text, and do not reproduce the heading error in the course.
- The EASA Easy Access version dated 2026-02-24 is the requested consolidated source, but it carries a non-authoritative-publication disclaimer. Preserve traceability to FCL.215 and ED Decision 2020/005/R, and run an amendment check before release.
- Regulation (EU) 2026/781 was checked because it post-dates the consolidation; no PPL theory-syllabus change was found. This does not remove the need to check later amendments.
- Live portals (AEMET, Rotax, BRS, Flight Design) are intentionally not frozen as course truth. The course should keep a controlled manifest containing URL, document identifier, revision/date, model/serial applicability and retrieval date for every type-specific lesson.
- No commercial flight school, blog, retailer, forum, unauthorised book copy or third-party mirror is accepted as evidence in this register.
